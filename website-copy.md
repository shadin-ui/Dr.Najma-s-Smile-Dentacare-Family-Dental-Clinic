## FAQs
**Page Objective:** Resolve concerns quickly and encourage booking.

### 1) Is dental treatment painful?
Most treatments are more comfortable than people expect with modern methods. We focus on gentle handling and proper pain control. If you are nervous, tell us — we will go slowly.

### 2) Is root canal treatment very painful?
This is a common concern. In many cases, RCT relieves existing severe pain and is done comfortably. We explain each step clearly so you feel prepared.

### 3) Will I know the cost before treatment starts?
Yes. After examination, we explain the plan and expected charges clearly so there are no surprises. Cost varies by case and treatment type within **[FEES RANGE]**.

### 4) What happens in the first appointment?
We listen to your concern, do a gentle check, and explain findings and options. You will understand next steps before deciding.

### 5) How long does one visit take?
Most consultations take around 20–40 minutes. Treatment visits may be longer based on your procedure.

### 6) Do you treat children?
Yes, we provide child-friendly care and guide parents at each step. We focus on making visits comfortable and positive.

### 7) I am very anxious. Can you help?
Absolutely. Many patients come with fear. We move at your pace, explain everything, and begin only when you are comfortable.

### 8) Do you maintain sterilisation and safety standards?
Yes. We follow strict sterilisation and hygiene protocols for instruments and treatment areas.

### 9) Do I need an appointment?
Appointments are best for shorter waiting and dedicated time. Walk-ins are possible based on availability.

### 10) Can I come for sudden tooth pain?
Yes. If you are in pain, call us immediately. We try our best to provide same-day attention whenever possible.

### 11) Do you explain treatment in simple language?
Always. We believe informed patients make better decisions. You will get clear, understandable guidance.

### 12) How often should I get a checkup?
For most people, every 6 months is ideal. Some patients may need more frequent visits based on gum health or cavity risk.

### 13) My gums bleed while brushing. Should I worry?
Bleeding gums should not be ignored. Early treatment usually works well and helps prevent bigger gum problems.

### 14) Do you offer smile improvement treatments?
Yes, we offer basic cosmetic options like whitening and smile refinement. We focus on natural, suitable results.
